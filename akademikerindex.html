Have you saved enough?
First of all, it is important that you find out if you have saved enough up for retirement. It's about setting a realistic budget and keeping it up against the expectations you have for life as a retiree. The budget must be an overall picture, where you include both pension and free funds.
If you are planning to sell your house or otherwise save, also remember to take that into account. However, be realistic in your valuation and pay attention to what options you have for taking out a loan in your house.
You should also be aware that it can be more difficult than before to be allowed to mortgage your deductible when you are retired and do not have a current income.
Does your coverage fit?
You can change some of your insurance coverage until you turn 60. For example, your spouse's / cohabitant's pension . You can change other insurances until you retire. For example, child pension . If you do not have a spouse or cohabitant or children under the age of 21, it may be beneficial to opt out of these insurance covers. Thereby you get yourself more in old age pension.
When the payout should begin
When you choose to retire, please contact us to begin the payout. We will send you a payout form, which we ask you to fill out and send to us no later than one month before you want the pension paid out. You can also choose to log in and use our self-service option
